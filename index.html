Data Visualization

I worked as a data designer for an atomic startup in Vancouver, BC, where I designed and built many interactive data dashboards in-house for our main client, Boeing.

I learned the true meaning of full stack here - conducting user research, working with database engineers on data and API definitions, wrangling data, sketching out chart specs and interactions, and building fully functional dashboards.


Outside of Boeing work, I also had a lot of fun creating data stories to promote our startup.


Here, I refined my own design and development process, learned to roll with the ambiguity and chaos that is startup life, and also leveled up in navigating the intricacies of a massive organization like Boeing.

Vision Science

At the University of British Columbia, I managed the Visual Cognition Lab and worked on research projects that explored interesting quirks with human visual perception.


On one project, we created data visualization using radical encodings such as luminance and orientation, to see whether people can effectively understand correlation from those unconventional charts.


I spent most of my time programming, performing statistical analysis, running experiments, and having a blast making data beautiful.
